# Competitive Analysis — Environmental Monitoring

## Water Quality Monitoring

### YSI Multi-Parameter Sondes
- **How it works:** Multiple electrochemical and optical sensors in a submersible housing
- **Cost:** $4,000-7,300
- **Parameters:** pH, dissolved oxygen, conductivity, turbidity, chlorophyll, nitrate (ISE)
- **Pros:** Submersible, real-time, multi-parameter, established
- **Cons:** Electrochemical sensors drift and need calibration. Limited to parameters with available electrode chemistry. Can't detect petroleum/hydrocarbons directly.

### ATR-FTIR (Benchtop)
- **How it works:** ATR crystal + Fourier Transform IR spectrometer
- **Cost:** $30,000-100,000
- **Pros:** Sees entire IR spectrum, identifies unknown contaminants, gold standard for lab analysis
- **Cons:** Lab-only, not field-deployable, expensive, requires trained operator, moving mirror maintenance

### Portable Oil-in-Water Analyzers
- **How it works:** Various (fluorescence, IR, gravimetric)
- **Cost:** $5,000-20,000
- **Pros:** Purpose-built, field-portable
- **Cons:** Single-analyte, some require solvents/consumables

### SFG Environmental Monitor (This Device)
- **Cost:** ~$16,000-30,000 (estimated)
- **Pros:** Multi-contaminant, solid-state, field-rugged, cheap silicon detectors
- **Cons:** Limited to pre-tuned contaminants, ATR adds complexity, longer-wavelength targets need alternative crystals

## Air Quality Monitoring

### Air Quality Monitoring Stations
- **Cost:** ~$10,000+ per station
- **Parameters:** PM2.5, PM10, O3, NO2, SO2, CO
- **Method:** Various (chemiluminescence, UV absorption, beta attenuation)
- **Pros:** Regulatory standard, well-characterized
- **Cons:** Bulky, high power consumption, expensive maintenance, point-measurement only

### Low-Cost Air Quality Sensors
- **Cost:** $100-500
- **Method:** Metal oxide semiconductor, electrochemical
- **Pros:** Cheap, small, IoT-compatible
- **Cons:** Poor accuracy, significant drift, cross-sensitivity, short lifespan

### SFG for Air Quality
- **Advantage:** Physics-based selectivity (no drift). Multi-gas. No consumable sensor elements.
- **Best fit:** Industrial fence-line VOC monitoring where accuracy matters more than cost.

## Market Size

- Water quality monitoring: $5.67B (2024), growing to $8.55B by 2030
- Air quality monitoring: $5.8B (2024)
- EPA monitors ~150 pollutants under Clean Water Act
- PFAS monitoring required by 2027, compliance by 2029
- Growing demand for continuous, real-time monitoring over periodic grab sampling
